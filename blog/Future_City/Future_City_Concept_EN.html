Core Concept 核心理念

This future city resolves three fundamental contradictions of modern urban life through radical vertical spatial separation: the conflict between people and vehicles, the separation between humans and nature, and the opposition between architecture and ecology.

The city is no longer a collection of fragments divided by roads. It becomes a continuous, organic, three-layered living entity. Machines descend below ground. Nature reclaims the surface. Humans live freely between sky and earth.

This is not an improvement of existing cities — it is a fundamental inversion of urban logic. Architecture no longer yields to roads. Nature is no longer severed by traffic. Humans no longer share the ground with vehicles. Each element finds its proper dimension, and the city achieves an unprecedented freedom.

Sky Layer — Low-Altitude Economy
SKY LAYER · LOW-ALTITUDE ECONOMY

Drones and eVTOL aircraft move freely above the city, all coordinated by a UTM system with zero human piloting. Rooftops serve as Vertiports and bird habitats simultaneously.

  • eVTOL air taxis: point-to-point short-haul travel
  • Drone logistics: packages to building rooftop receivers
  • City management drones: security, inspection, ecological monitoring
  • Every building has a Vertiport on the roof, for both drones and birds
🌳
Ground Layer — Living Ecosystem
GROUND LAYER · LIVING ECOSYSTEM

Roads vanish entirely. The surface becomes a continuous, unbroken whole — no cars, no fences, no setbacks. Buildings connect freely; humans and animals share the land.

  • Large ecological green space, woods, waterways
  • Open urban wildlife habitat: humans enter animals’ territory
  • Slow mobility zone: walking, strollers, skateboards
  • Transit Portal plazas: gateways to the underground
🚗
Underground Layer — Automated Transport
UNDERGROUND · AUTOMATED TRANSPORT

All motorized transport moves underground, fully autonomous. The underground drone corridor is a world first — sealed tunnels, no wind or rain, stable flight, city-wide delivery.

  • Fully autonomous cars and buses
  • Underground drone logistics corridors (Sub-Surface UAV Corridor)
  • Metro and rapid rail
  • Surface highways remain outside the city for intercity travel

Solving the Core Challenge of Autonomous Driving 自动驾驶的核心难题

The greatest unsolved challenge in autonomous driving today is the unpredictability of human pedestrian behavior — people change direction suddenly, get distracted, jaywalk. No algorithm can perfectly anticipate a human’s next step.

This city sidesteps the problem with architectural elegance: physical separation.

The ground layer contains only people. The underground layer contains only machines. The two never meet. The underground system only needs to coordinate machine-to-machine interactions — uncertainty drops to near zero, and both efficiency and safety rise dramatically. This is not a technological breakthrough. It is the wisdom of spatial design.

All vehicles and drones connect to a central control system (analogous to UTM). Users open an app, request a vehicle or drone, and the system handles everything.

Animal Coexistence & Symbiotic Architecture 动物共存与共生建筑

This city is not “a park with animals.” It is “a natural habitat that includes humans.” Humans are guests; animals are the hosts. This inverts the power logic of the traditional zoo entirely — no fences, no boundaries; the whole city is the habitat.

Buildings are not “houses with greenery.” They are part of the ecosystem itself. Facades are living plant systems; birds and insects nest in the walls. Forms mimic the organic structure of tree roots, honeycombs, and coral reefs.

Key Challenges & Considerations 现实挑战

Every great vision must face its challenges. Here are the questions this city must answer:

  • 01
    Cybersecurity risk If the central control system is compromised, the entire city’s transport could fail. Decentralized failover and redundancy are essential.
  • 02
    Construction cost Boston’s Big Dig overran by 97%, reaching $14.6 billion. City-scale underground construction would be far larger, requiring new financing and delivery models.
  • 03
    Disease & sanitation risk Dense human–animal cohabitation has historically been a vector for disease. Real-time ecological health monitoring is non-negotiable.
  • 04
    Species selection Large predators cannot safely coexist with humans. Scientific criteria must govern which species and ecological structure are introduced.
  • 05
    Underground drone corridors No global precedent exists. Ventilation, electromagnetic interference, and maintenance need further research and prototyping.
  • 06
    Social equity How does such a high-tech city ensure that all income levels benefit equally?

Full Concept Statement 完整概念陈述

This city operates across three vertical layers. Sky layer: drones and eVTOL aircraft are coordinated by a UTM system with zero human piloting, handling delivery and short-haul travel. Ground layer: roads have vanished; the surface is a continuous ecosystem — woods, waterways, open wildlife habitat and buildings form one organic whole; humans are guests, not masters; facades are living plants, forms mimic nature, ground floors are fully open so animals move through freely, rooftops are Vertiports and bird habitat. Underground layer: all motorized transport runs below ground, fully autonomous, plus underground drone logistics corridors — sealed tunnels for stable flight and city-wide delivery. The three layers connect via Vertical Mobility Hubs; building basements connect to lanes; users request a ride in one tap and the system does the rest. Surface highways remain outside the city for intercity travel.

The city’s greatness lies not in any single technological breakthrough, but in the redesign of spatial logic — through vertical layering, giving humans, nature, and machines each their proper place, so the city becomes a true symbiosis.
